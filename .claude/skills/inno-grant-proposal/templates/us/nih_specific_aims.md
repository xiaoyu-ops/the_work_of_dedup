## Annotated Example (anonymized, for structural reference only)

<!-- This example shows the STRUCTURE and FLOW of a strong Specific Aims page.
     The science is fictional. Focus on how each element connects. -->

> **Opening:** Alzheimer's disease (AD) affects 6.7 million Americans and costs $345 billion annually in care, yet no disease-modifying therapy exists. A critical barrier to progress is our inability to detect neurodegenerative changes before irreversible neuronal loss occurs. **The long-term goal** of our research program is to develop non-invasive biomarkers for early AD detection. **The central hypothesis** is that retinal microvascular changes, detectable by optical coherence tomography angiography (OCTA), mirror cerebrovascular pathology and precede cognitive decline by 5-10 years. This hypothesis is supported by our preliminary data showing significant retinal capillary density reduction (p<0.001, n=120) in pre-symptomatic APOE4 carriers compared to matched controls. We are uniquely positioned to pursue this work given our established cohort of 800 longitudinally-followed at-risk individuals and our expertise in computational retinal image analysis.
>
> **Aim 1: Characterize** the relationship between retinal microvascular changes and established cerebrospinal fluid AD biomarkers in 400 participants across the AD continuum. *Hypothesis:* Retinal capillary density correlates inversely with CSF p-tau/Aβ42 ratio (r > 0.4). *Approach:* Cross-sectional OCTA imaging paired with lumbar puncture. *Expected outcomes:* Quantified sensitivity and specificity of retinal biomarkers for AD staging.
>
> **Aim 2: Determine** whether retinal microvascular changes predict cognitive decline over a 3-year follow-up period in 300 cognitively normal at-risk individuals. *Hypothesis:* Baseline retinal capillary density predicts conversion to MCI with AUC > 0.75. *Approach:* Longitudinal OCTA at 6-month intervals with annual neuropsychological testing.
>
> **Aim 3: Develop** a machine learning model integrating retinal, genetic, and demographic features for individualized AD risk prediction. *Hypothesis:* The multimodal model outperforms any single biomarker (ΔAUC ≥ 0.10). *Approach:* Deep learning on retinal images combined with polygenic risk scores.
>
> **Payoff:** Completion of these aims will establish retinal imaging as a low-cost, non-invasive screening tool for preclinical AD — transforming early detection from an invasive, expensive procedure to a 5-minute eye scan accessible in any optometrist's office.
