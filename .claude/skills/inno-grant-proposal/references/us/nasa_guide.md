# NASA Grant Writing Guide

## Agency Overview

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) funds research across Earth and space science, technology development, and aeronautics. Research funding is primarily managed through the Science Mission Directorate (SMD), which is organized into five divisions:

**Earth Science Division (ESD)**
- Earth's climate, weather, natural hazards, and global change
- Heavy emphasis on NASA satellite data (Landsat, MODIS, ICESat-2, GRACE, etc.)
- Funding: ROSES Earth Science solicitations, FINESST, Applied Sciences

**Planetary Science Division (PSD)**
- Solar system exploration, planetary geology, astrobiology
- Mission-linked research and data analysis
- Funding: ROSES Planetary Science solicitations, FINESST

**Astrophysics Division**
- Origin and evolution of the universe, exoplanets, fundamental physics
- Data from Hubble, JWST, Chandra, and other observatories
- Funding: ROSES Astrophysics solicitations, FINESST

**Heliophysics Division**
- Sun-Earth connection, space weather, solar physics
- Data from SOHO, SDO, Parker Solar Probe, and others
- Funding: ROSES Heliophysics solicitations, FINESST

**Biological and Physical Sciences Division**
- Life and physical sciences in space environments
- Research aboard the International Space Station
- Funding: Selected ROSES calls, FINESST (some cycles)

## Program Types and Budget Ranges

### ROSES (Research Opportunities in Space and Earth Sciences)
The primary annual omnibus solicitation for NASA research. ROSES contains dozens of individual program elements released throughout the year, each with its own scope, budget, and deadline.

- **Research and Analysis (R&A)**: Core investigator-initiated research; $100K--$300K/year for 2--3 years
- **Data Analysis Programs**: Analysis of data from specific missions; $80K--$200K/year
- **Instrument Development**: Technology for future missions; $200K--$1M/year
- **Interdisciplinary Research**: Cross-division studies; varies widely

### FINESST (Future Investigators in NASA Earth and Space Science and Technology)
Graduate student research fellowships. See detailed section below.
- Up to $50,000/year for up to 3 years
- Student (Future Investigator) leads the research; advisor serves as PI
- Approximately 15% selection rate

### NIAC (NASA Innovative Advanced Concepts)
Visionary, far-term aerospace concepts.
- **Phase I**: $175K for 9 months (feasibility study)
- **Phase II**: $600K for 2 years (detailed study)
- **Phase III**: $2M for 2 years (concept maturation)

### STTR/SBIR (Small Business Programs)
Technology development with small businesses.
- **SBIR Phase I**: Up to $150K for 6 months
- **SBIR Phase II**: Up to $750K for 24 months
- **STTR**: Similar structure but requires research institution partnership

## FINESST: Detailed Guide

FINESST is NASA's primary graduate student fellowship program. Based on successful applicant insights and official solicitation requirements, here is a comprehensive breakdown.

### Proposal Components

**1. Proposal Summary (NSPIRES cover page)** -- Anonymized
- Brief overview of the research on the cover page

**2. Science/Technical/Management (STM) Section (6 pages)** -- Anonymized
- Background and research questions
- Proposed methods and analysis plan
- Contingency plans for potential challenges
- NASA mission/data relevance (critical for Earth Science)
- Timeline with milestones
- This is the core of the proposal; reviewers spend most time here

**3. References and Acknowledgments** -- Anonymized, no page limit
- Use numbered citation style [1], [2] (not author-year) to maintain anonymity
- Do not include acknowledgments that reveal identity

**4. Open Science and Data Management Plan (OSDMP) (2 pages)** -- Anonymized
- How data will be archived and shared
- Software and code sharing plans
- Publication access plans (open access)
- Alignment with NASA's Open Science policies (SPD-41a)

**5. Mentoring Plan or Agreement (2 pages)** -- Anonymized
- How the PI will mentor the Future Investigator
- Professional development activities
- Meeting frequency and communication plan
- Career development milestones

**6. Research Readiness Statement (1 page)** -- NOT anonymized
- The Future Investigator's background and qualifications
- Relevant coursework, skills, and experience
- Why the FI is prepared to execute this research
- This is where reviewers learn who you are

**7. Biosketches (PI and FI)** -- NOT anonymized
- NASA-format biosketches for both advisor and student

**8. Current and Pending Support** -- NOT anonymized
- All active and pending funding for both PI and FI

**9. Budget and Budget Narrative (~2 pages)** -- NOT anonymized
- Detailed budget within the $50K/year cap
- Justify all expenses (stipend, tuition, travel, supplies, computing)

### Dual Anonymous Peer Review (DAPR)

FINESST uses NASA's Dual Anonymous Peer Review system. This is one of the most critical aspects to get right.

**Core Rules:**
- Reviewers do not see who wrote the proposal (anonymized sections)
- Proposers do not know who reviews the proposal
- Violations of anonymity can result in proposal rejection

**What You CANNOT Include in Anonymized Sections:**
- Your name or your advisor's name
- Your university or institution name
- Names of specific collaborators (say "a NASA collaborator" not "Dr. Smith at JPL")
- Self-citations in author-year format (use numbered references)
- References to "our lab" or "my previous work" in ways that reveal identity
- Mention of unique facilities or instruments that identify your institution
- Acknowledgments of specific named individuals

**What You CAN Include:**
- Institution types (e.g., "a large research university," "one of the DOE national labs")
- General expertise descriptions
- Collaboration types without names
- Numbered citations to your own work (if written in third person)
- General descriptions of available resources

**Practical Tips for Anonymization:**
- Write in third person throughout the STM
- Replace "we will" with "this study will" or "the proposed research will"
- Reference your own publications as "[Author, Year]" using numbered format: "Previous work [14] demonstrated..."
- Describe facilities generically: "a scanning electron microscope facility" not "the MIT CMSE facility"
- Have someone unfamiliar with your work read the proposal to check for identifying information

## Review Criteria

NASA evaluates FINESST proposals on four main criteria:

### 1. Scientific/Technical Merit
- Significance of the research question
- Innovation and originality of the approach
- Soundness of the methodology
- Feasibility within the proposed timeline
- Quality of preliminary work or conceptual development

### 2. Relevance to NASA
- Connection to NASA's strategic goals and missions
- Use of NASA data, instruments, or facilities
- Contribution to NASA's science objectives
- For Earth Science: clear use of NASA satellite data or airborne campaign data

### 3. Research Readiness
- FI's preparation and qualifications
- PI's mentoring track record
- Institutional support and resources
- Feasibility of the timeline given the FI's stage in their program
- Quality of the mentoring plan

### 4. Cost Reasonableness
- Budget clarity and justification
- Adherence to the $50K/year limit
- Appropriate allocation across categories
- No excessive or unjustified costs

Proposals receive adjectival ratings (Excellent, Very Good, Good, Fair, Poor) with written reviewer comments.

## Budget Rules

### FINESST Budget
- Maximum $50,000 per year, up to 3 years ($150K total)
- Covers: student stipend/salary, tuition and fees, health insurance, research supplies, travel, computing, publication costs
- Indirect costs are included in the $50K cap (this significantly reduces the available direct costs)
- Travel should include at least one scientific conference per year
- Budget for computing resources if needed (cloud computing, HPC time)

### General NASA ROSES Budget
- Varies by program element; check the specific solicitation
- Most programs allow standard university indirect cost rates
- Equipment purchases over $5,000 require specific justification
- Foreign travel requires additional justification
- Subcontracts to other institutions are allowed but must be justified

### Budget Tips
- Calculate your institution's indirect cost rate impact early; a 55% rate on a $50K cap means only ~$32K in direct costs
- Prioritize: student support first, then research essentials, then travel
- If tuition is high, discuss cost-sharing options with your department

## Common Mistakes

1. **Breaking DAPR anonymity**: The single most common fatal error. Any identifying information in anonymized sections can lead to rejection.

2. **Weak NASA relevance**: Especially for Earth Science, proposals must clearly specify which NASA datasets will be used and how. Generic mentions of "satellite data" are insufficient.

3. **Overly ambitious scope**: Proposing a 3-year project that would challenge a postdoc team. Scope must be achievable by one graduate student.

4. **Poor Research Readiness Statement**: This 1-page document is your only chance to tell reviewers who you are. Make it compelling -- highlight relevant skills, coursework, and preliminary experience.

5. **Ignoring the OSDMP**: The Open Science and Data Management Plan is reviewed. A generic or vague plan signals lack of preparation.

6. **Neglecting the Mentoring Plan**: Reviewers want to see a thoughtful plan for professional development, not a boilerplate statement.

7. **Budget errors**: Forgetting that indirect costs eat into the $50K cap; requesting items not allowable under NASA policy.

8. **Missing the timeline**: Not showing a realistic schedule for completing the work within the degree timeline.

9. **Weak methodology section**: Describing what you will study but not how. Include specific methods, tools, datasets, and analysis approaches.

10. **Not reading the solicitation carefully**: FINESST requirements change slightly each year. Always read the current year's solicitation document completely.

## Tips for Satellite Data Integration (Earth Science)

Earth Science FINESST proposals must demonstrate clear use of NASA data. Here is how to strengthen this aspect:

### Specify Your Data Sources
- Name the exact NASA missions and instruments: "MODIS Terra Collection 6.1 Level 2 aerosol optical depth product (MOD04_L2)"
- Identify the data archive: "obtained from NASA's Earthdata portal (LP DAAC)"
- Describe the spatial and temporal resolution and coverage you need
- Explain why NASA data (vs. other sources) is essential for your research

### Multi-Sensor Approaches
- Combining multiple NASA datasets strengthens NASA relevance
- Example: integrating Landsat (spatial detail) with MODIS (temporal frequency) and ICESat-2 (vertical structure)
- Explain the complementary strengths of each dataset

### Validation Strategy
- Describe how you will validate satellite-derived results
- Mention ground-truth data sources (AmeriFlux, AERONET, field campaigns)
- Propose uncertainty quantification approaches
- If using NASA airborne campaign data for validation, name the campaign

### Data Processing Pipeline
- Describe your data processing workflow
- Mention relevant tools: Google Earth Engine, NASA AppEEARS, Giovanni, or custom code
- Show you understand the data (preprocessing steps, quality flags, known limitations)
- Address data volume and computational requirements

### Connect to NASA Missions
- Tie your research to current or upcoming NASA missions
- Reference NASA's Earth Science strategic plan and decadal survey priorities
- Show how your work supports mission science objectives
- If your methods could apply to future missions (e.g., SBG, NISAR, PACE), mention this

## ROSES General Proposal Tips

For non-FINESST ROSES proposals:

- **Read the specific program element carefully**: Each ROSES element has unique requirements, page limits, and review criteria
- **Check the NASA Science Plan**: Align your proposal with current strategic priorities
- **Contact the program officer**: NASA program officers are generally responsive and can clarify scope and fit
- **Dual-anonymous considerations**: Many (but not all) ROSES elements now use DAPR; check the specific solicitation
- **Step-1 proposals**: Some ROSES elements require a brief Step-1 proposal before the full submission; do not skip this
- **NOI (Notice of Intent)**: When requested, always submit one; it helps NASA plan the review
